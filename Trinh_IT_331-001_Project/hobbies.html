Muay Thai

One of my hobbies is training in combat sports. I currently go to a local MMA gym called Capital MMA where I train in Muay Thai and Jiu Jitsu. I think martial arts is a big part of my life, as I grew up training in Tae Kwon Do and also eventually becomming an instructor for the same sport.

Muay Thai is a form of boxing that originated from Thailand. What makes this sport unique is the usage of knees and elbows. It is for this reason why we call Muay Thai the art of the 8 limbs. Your arms, legs, knees, and elbows.

Jiu Jitsu

Jiu Jitsu, on the other hand, is a completely different beast. This form of martial arts relies on technical skill in order to submit your opponent.There are two variations for jiu jitsu, gi and no gi. A gi is basically the uniform that students wear. No gi jiu jitsu is commonly seen in large fight entertainment organizations, such as the UFC.

Personally, my favorite form of jiu jitsu is no gi. Mostly because it provides a more realistic scenario, should the situation ever arrive that you need to defend yourself in reality. To people who don't train, this sport might seem a bit boring because there is no striking involved. However, high levels cannot be achieved without years of experience and mastery of fundamentals.
